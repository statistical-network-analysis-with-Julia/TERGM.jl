Simulation

simulate_stergm draws one transition:

  1. sample $Y^+$ by Metropolis toggles over the non-edges of $Y_{t-1}$ under the formation coefficients ($Y^+$ always contains $Y_{t-1}$);
  2. sample $Y^-$ by toggles over the edges of $Y_{t-1}$ under the dissolution coefficients ($Y^-$ is always contained in $Y_{t-1}$);
  3. combine: $Y_t = (Y^+ \setminus Y_{t-1}) \cup Y^-$.

simulate_network_sequence iterates transitions; simulate_stergm(result, n_steps) continues from the last observed panel. gof — TERGM.jl's method on the shared Networks.gof generic, returning a Networks.GOFResult (stergm_gof is a backward-compatible alias) — compares observed formed/persisted tie counts per transition against their simulated distributions with two-sided Monte Carlo p-values.
